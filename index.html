How to Make a Potato Frittata

By Kristin Wolf

Step 1: Gather the Ingredients

You will need:

  • 6 eggs
  • 2 medium potatoes, diced
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese
  • 2 Tbsps olive oil
  • 1 tsp. salt & 1/4 tsp. pepper
Optional: 1/2 cup chopped vegetables such as Swiss chard, tomatoes, or peppers

Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables for Saute

Chop the onions and garlic, set aside.

Chop the potatos into cubes (1/2 inch, approx).

If adding additional vegetables, prepare to add at the appropriate time, during saute.(Add peppers with potatoes, add chopped greens during last few minutes).

Step 3: Prepare the Eggs

Preheat the oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit (you'll be baking the eggs).

Prepare a 13x9 inch baking pan by coating it with non-stick spray or olive oil.

Crack the eggs into a bowl. Season with salt and pepper. Wisk just until blended, set aside.

Step 4: Saute the Vegetables

Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.

Add onions, season with salt and pepper. Saute until onions are softening, add garlic.

Add potatos, stirring occasionally, until done. Add any addt'l vegetables according to cooking time.

Step 5: Layer the Ingredients in a Baking Dish

Transfer the vegetables to the baking dish and spread them evenly across the bottom.

Pour the wisked eggs over the vegetables. Use a spoon to ensure that all vegetables are glazed.

Use the shredded cheese to cover the egg and vegetable mixture in a thin layer.

Step 6: Bake and Enjoy!

Place pan in center of the preheated oven and cook for 11-12 minutes. The cheese should be melted, but not brown.

Remove the pan from the oven when the eggs are cooked through. Allow the frittata to rest for two minutes, then slice and serve.

Garnish with fresh greens, sliced tomatoes, or fruit. Enjoy!
